## Post 1

**Date:** Early 2025  

> **Good prompt tracking starts with sample design.**
>
> For your average B2B SaaS CRM category, I’d start with ~40 seed prompts: 12 brand, 12 category, and 16 problem prompts. The problem prompts matter because that is where purchase intent usually lives.
>
> Then I’d run each prompt 5x per platform every week. ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, and Google AI Overviews should each get their own score because aggregation hides how differently the engines behave.
>
> The panel also needs personas. CFO, IT, and marketing buyers evaluate the same CRM category through different criteria, so averaging them together hides where a brand actually wins or loses.
>
> The metric set should include mention rate, citation rate, average position, sentiment, and the attributes attached to each mention, all with confidence intervals.
>
> That is how prompt tracking becomes more accurate: each answer becomes one sample in a designed panel, not one truth in a dashboard.
>
> More guidance in this week's Growth Memo.

**Key Insight for AI-SEO Production:** This gives 100Hires a literal, step-by-step measurement framework for Generative Engine Optimization (GEO). Instead of traditional rank tracking, we need to build a "panel" of ~40 prompts (e.g., "What is 100Hires?", "Best ATS for startups," "How to reduce time-to-hire") and track our *mention rate* and *sentiment* across ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini weekly.

## Post 2

**Date:** Early 2025  

> **AI answers are probabilistic, but prompt tracking can still become more accurate.**
>
> The backlash against prompt tracking gets one thing right: one prompt run is not enough. 5 people can run the same prompt and get 5 different answers.
>
> The wrong conclusion is that prompt tracking is useless. The better conclusion is that prompt tracking needs better sampling.
>
> Run prompts multiple times. Keep platform scores separate. Add confidence intervals. Track personas and follow-up journeys. Store raw answers so you can audit whether the issue is the model, the scorer, the scraper, or the prompt.
>
> Prompt tracking will not become keyword tracking. It will look more like polling.
>
> Full breakdown in this week's Growth Memo.

**Key Insight for AI-SEO Production:** When setting expectations with the 100Hires CEO about our AI-SEO performance, we must communicate that "ranking in ChatGPT" is not a fixed state like "Ranking #1 on Google." We must present our success metrics like political polling: "We have a 75% citation rate across 5 runs on Perplexity for the query 'affordable ATS alternatives'."

## Post 3

**Date:** Early 2025  

> **47.5% of all scrolling on AIO SERPs goes backward, compared to 27% without an AIO on the page.**
>
> Among users who reverse direction on an AIO SERP, the median user spends nearly half their total scroll going back up the page.
>
> Scrolling stopped being a one-way trip. On an AIO SERP, the user reads, weighs, scrolls past, then circles back to re-read.
>
> The implication for meta descriptions: your listing is no longer getting one impression. It's getting 2, sometimes 3. The first impression earns the read on the way down. The second impression earns the click on the way back up.
>
> The tactical implication: think about how to make your meta title and description scroll-stopping without sacrificing trust.
>
> Full analysis in this week's Growth Memo.

**Key Insight for AI-SEO Production:** Because Google's AI Overviews (AIO) change how users physically interact with the search results page (scrolling down past the AIO, then back up), our Meta Titles and Descriptions for 100Hires need to be engineered to capture attention on the "second pass." They must stand out visually and contextually against the generic AI summary at the top of the page.
