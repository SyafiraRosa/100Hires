> **How to write content that AI systems actually quote (not just index)**
>
> I spent the last year studying how LLMs select content for citations.
>
> Here is what I found:
>
> LLMs don't cite entire pages. They cite **specific sentences and paragraphs.**
>
> And the sentences they prefer have a very specific structure:
>
> ✅ **Definitional**: "X is Y" — direct, unambiguous definitions
> ✅ **Statistical**: "Studies show that X increases Y by Z%" — specific quantified claims
> ✅ **Procedural**: "To do X, follow steps A, B, C" — clear, ordered instructions
> ✅ **Comparative**: "Unlike X, Y does Z" — comparative framing that AI can use to answer "X vs Y" questions
> ✅ **Authoritative attribution**: "According to [Named Expert], X is Y" — AI systems prefer attributed claims
>
> ❌ **What AI does NOT cite well:**
> Long narrative paragraphs where the key insight is buried in the middle
> Vague qualitative statements ("many experts believe X")
> Content written primarily for humans to read narratively, not for machines to parse structurally
>
> If you want your content to be quoted by AI, write content that reads like a structured answer, not a story.

**Key Insight for AI-SEO Production:** This is the most tactical, sentence-level writing guideline in the research set. 100Hires content should be audited against Britney's citation-friendly sentence framework: every key point should be written as a definitional, statistical, or procedural statement — not buried in narrative prose.

> **The ML concept that every content marketer needs to understand: Chunking**
>
> When a language model processes your content, it doesn't read it the way a human does.
>
> It processes it in "chunks" — typically ~512 tokens (roughly 350-400 words) per segment.
>
> Each chunk is embedded as a vector and stored in the model's retrieval system.
>
> When someone asks a question, the model retrieves the most relevant chunks from its knowledge base.
>
> What this means for your content:
>
> 1. **Every section should be self-contained** — a reader (or LLM) jumping to any chunk of your page should understand the key point without needing to read the previous section
>
> 2. **Key information must appear in the first 100-150 words of each section** — not at the end
>
> 3. **Headers are extremely important for chunking** — they help the model understand where one concept ends and another begins
>
> 4. **Tables and lists chunk cleanly** — prose that wraps across chunk boundaries gets disrupted
>
> 5. **Repeat key entities and concepts in each section** — don't assume the model remembers context from 500 words earlier
>
> Understanding chunking is the difference between writing content that AI retrieves and writing content that AI misses.

**Key Insight for AI-SEO Production:** Britney's "chunking" framework provides a technical writing standard for 100Hires content. Every AI-assisted article should be structured so that any ~400-word section stands alone as a useful, complete answer — with key information front-loaded in each section.

## Post 3

> **The practical AI toolkit I actually use for SEO content in 2025:**
>
> After testing dozens of tools, here is what I actually keep in rotation:
>
> **Research Phase:**
> - Perplexity Pro — for quick topic research with cited sources
> - Claude — for synthesizing research and identifying gaps
> - Ahrefs — for keyword and competitor gap analysis
>
> **Brief Creation:**
> - Claude + custom system prompt — generates detailed, entity-rich briefs when given SERP context
>
> **Drafting:**
> - Claude (long-form) — best for structural coherence over long documents
> - GPT-4o — best for varying tone and creative takes on standard frameworks
>
> **Fact-Checking:**
> - Manual verification for all statistics (no tool replaces this)
> - Perplexity for quick claim verification with source citation
>
> **NLP Optimization:**
> - Clearscope — entity coverage audit
> - SurferSEO — on-page scoring against top competitors
>
> **Structured Data:**
> - Schema.org generator + manual validation via Google's Rich Results Test
>
> The most important tool in this stack is human judgment applied at every stage.
> AI accelerates. Humans decide.

**Key Insight for AI-SEO Production:** This toolkit is directly usable as a technology stack recommendation for 100Hires' AI content production program. It validates the use of Claude (already required in the job spec) and provides specific tool recommendations for each phase of the production workflow.
