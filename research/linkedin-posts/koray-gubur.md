## Post 1

**Date:** Early 2025  

> **What is an Authority Signature?**
>
> Google has a concept called “Authority Signature,” which appears in a patent that carries “Topic Authority” in its name.
>
> The patent “Determining Topic Authority” explains that an author’s expertise, and the author’s signature on a document associated with a specific topic, can increase the topical authority of that document.
>
> In other words, Google can evaluate not only the document and the website, but also the person-like entity behind the content.
>
> This becomes even more interesting today because Google is reintroducing famous “authors,” “people,” and “answers” in a social-like format through Google Profiles.
>
> Google can recognize person-like entities, gather information about them under a specific profile, and define them with expertise signals.
>
> According to the patent, the simplified formula for Authority Signature is:
> Authority Signature Update = Authorship Percentage of Author A for Topic T in Document D × Topical Weight of Topic T in Document D
>
> [Continues with mathematical example of authority calculation...]
>
> This means the system does not only evaluate whether an author exists. It can also evaluate:
> - Which topic the author contributes to
> - How much of the document belongs to that topic
> - How much of that topical section the author actually authored
> - How consistently the author contributes to the same topic across different documents
>
> This is important because the patent mainly defines topic authority for authors, not websites.
>
> Topical Authority is not only about how much a website covers a topic. It is also about who covers the topic, how consistently they cover it, and whether the system can recognize the author as a real entity with a measurable topical signature.

**Key Insight for AI-SEO Production:** For 100Hires, AI-generated content cannot be published anonymously or under a generic "100Hires Team" byline. To build true Topical Authority, content must be attributed to real, verifiable "person-like entities" (e.g., Alex Kravets, CEO) who consistently publish on specific recruitment/ATS topics. AI should be used to draft, but the "Authority Signature" must belong to a real expert.

## Post 2

**Date:** Early 2025  

> **A luxury travel brand’s last 6 months of SEO results:**
>
> Total clicks: +46.7% (24K vs 16.4K)
> Total impressions: +123.7% (5.26M vs 2.35M)
> Average CTR: -28.6% (0.5% vs 0.7%)
> Average position: +58.2% improvement (9.4 vs 22.5)
>
> This is the 3rd travel industry project we are sharing, and it focuses on a highly niche-specific variation of the travel market.
>
> The company’s main obstacle is competing against highly authoritative provider brands, while large aggregators leverage forum and UGC content with limited visual semantics.
>
> In this type of market, the website still has to rank for commercial terms. Thus, we heavily focused on the informational segment of the topical map and used it to support the commercial sections through contextual internal linking.
>
> During the project, no backlink-related progress was implemented.
>
> According to Microsoft Bing data, the website received over 500,000 impressions from AI search features, along with 28,000 citations.
>
> Let’s use “safari” as a replacement example for the actual industry. If the central entity is “safari,” you need to cover all possible attributes and relational contexts around it, such as: Safari + vaccines, Safari + luggage, Safari + seasons, etc.
>
> All possible predicates and relations around the safari context should be organized as a connected content network. Commercial internal links should point to the review pages, main provider pages, itineraries, routes, and engagement-focused sections.
>
> A commercial page can rank better temporarily, but it won’t always sustain long-term rankings if the necessary visual and functional elements are missing.

**Key Insight for AI-SEO Production:** This provides the structural blueprint for 100Hires. We must use AI to rapidly build out the "informational segment of the topical map" (e.g., hiring laws, interview questions, recruitment metrics) solely to act as an internal linking engine that passes semantic relevance to our core commercial pages (100Hires vs Competitor comparison pages).

## Post 3

**Date:** Early 2025  

> **Good news for SEOs: Google is bringing Generative AI Performance reporting to Google Search Console.**
>
> At first, it will mainly help us track AI appearances and impressions, but this is already a meaningful step forward.
>
> Why? Because we can finally start separating traditional web-result impressions from AI-result impressions more clearly.
>
> For Holistic SEOs, this opens many new analytical layers:
> - Compare crawl stats and specific user agents with AI appearances.
> - Compare sentence structures, content freshness, and source responsiveness against AI visibility.
> - Compare AI performance with the average position of commercial queries.
> - Compare free-tool query segments with AI appearances to understand whether helpful utility-based assets support broader brand and topical relevance.
>
> For now, the report may not feel fully satisfying because Google is mostly giving impression-level data.
>
> But even with this limitation, there are many new metrics, patterns, and signals that a Holistic SEO can create from it.
>
> AI visibility is not only about being mentioned. It is about understanding why a source is selected, when it is selected, and which semantic, technical, and behavioral signals support that selection.

**Key Insight for AI-SEO Production:** This dictates how 100Hires will measure the success of the AI content program. We will not just track traditional rankings; we will actively monitor the "Generative AI Performance" reports to reverse-engineer which sentence structures and content formats (lists, tables, specific stats) trigger AI citations for our target queries.
