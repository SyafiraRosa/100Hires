## Post 1

**Date:** October 2024  
**Engagement:** ~2,340 reactions, 254 comments  

> **Before you use AI to write any SEO content, do this first:**
>
> 1. **Define the target query cluster** — not just one keyword, but the group of semantically related queries this page should capture
> 2. **Analyze the SERP composition** — what types of content are ranking? Blog posts? Tools? Comparison pages? The format tells you what intent is being satisfied.
> 3. **Identify the entities** — what people, places, products, and concepts are consistently mentioned across top-ranking pages?
> 4. **Map the content gap** — what's missing from the top 10 results that a user would still need?
> 5. **Define the unique angle** — what can your content add that the top 10 don't have?
>
> THEN give this structured context to your AI tool.
>
> The output quality difference between a bare "write me an article about X" prompt and a context-rich structured prompt is not 2x better. It is 10–20x better.
>
> AI is a force multiplier for research that has already been done. Not a replacement for research itself.

**Key Insight for AI-SEO Production:** Aleyda's 5-step pre-production checklist is directly adaptable as a Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) for 100Hires' content production pipeline. Every AI content brief should include all five elements before the writing stage begins.

## Post 2

**Date:** January 2025

> **The SEOFOMO audit of 2025: What's actually working in AI SEO right now**
>
> I have been synthesizing hundreds of SEO experiments from the community for my newsletter and here's what the data consistently shows:
>
> **What is working:**
> - Comparison and alternative pages ("X vs Y", "Best X for [use case]") — these have extremely high commercial intent and AI systems cite them heavily
> - FAQ-structured content that answers specific sub-questions in a discrete, self-contained way
> - Content that synthesizes multiple data sources into a new framework (not just summarizing them)
> - Author bio pages with verifiable credentials and external references
>
> **What is not working:**
> - Thin AI-generated content that covers a topic at "definition" level without going deeper
> - Programmatic pages with templated structure and no differentiation
> - Content that targets queries with low commercial intent in an attempt to build "awareness"
>
> The pattern is consistent: **depth + specificity + commercial intent = success in 2025.**
>
> Surface-level awareness content is now permanently Google's domain (AI Overviews). Anything left for organic search requires genuine expertise and purchase intent alignment.

**Key Insight for AI-SEO Production:** The "what is working" list is a direct content calendar recommendation for 100Hires. Comparison pages, alternative pages, and FAQ-structured use-case content should be the top priorities for AI-assisted content production in 2025.

## Post 3

**Date:** April 2025  
**Engagement:** ~2,100 reactions, 231 comments  

> **The AI SEO workflow that I actually use for client projects:**
>
> **Phase 1: Research (Human-Led)**
> - SERP analysis: identify content types, intent signals, and entity patterns
> - Competitor gap analysis: what are they ranking for that you are not?
> - Topical map review: what content does the site need to establish authority?
>
> **Phase 2: Brief Creation (Human + AI)**
> - Use AI to generate a detailed content brief template
> - Manually validate: target entities, search intent alignment, unique angle
> - Add all context the AI writer will need: tone, audience, competitive differentiation
>
> **Phase 3: Drafting (AI-Led with Human Context)**
> - Feed the validated brief to AI for first draft
> - AI handles structure, standard facts, and initial framing
>
> **Phase 4: Refinement (Human-Led)**
> - Inject original data, expert quotes, and proprietary insights
> - Verify all factual claims (especially statistics, dates, product specs)
> - Align with E-E-A-T signals: author bio, source citations, real-world examples
>
> **Phase 5: Technical SEO (Human-Led)**
> - Add structured data markup
> - Optimize internal linking
> - Verify entity coverage with NLP audit
>
> Total time for a 2,000-word high-quality piece: ~3 hours vs. ~8–10 hours without AI.
> Quality outcome: equivalent or higher than fully human-written with equivalent expertise.
>
> This is what AI-powered SEO content production actually looks like at a professional level.

**Key Insight for AI-SEO Production:** This 5-phase workflow is the most production-ready AI content framework in the research set. It is directly usable as 100Hires' standard content production SOP, with minimal adaptation needed.
