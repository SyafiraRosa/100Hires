## Post 1

**Date:** Early 2026  

> **I'm seeing a trend of SEOs calling a chart like this a failure. This is an absolute win!**
>
> This is a programmatic project on Ahrefs. It took a hit, but it still drives nearly 1M organic and 4.5M total visits a month. It's down from it's peak, but it's a lot higher than the 0 it was 3 years ago. Plus these pages are actually useful for users, which is why only a portion of the traffic is from organic search.

If we launch an AI-driven programmatic SEO campaign (e.g., generating thousands of localized "Recruiting in [City]" pages), we must define success absolutely, not relatively. An algorithm update might shave off 20% of traffic, but if the baseline was 0 and we are now driving 10,000 qualified leads, the project is a massive ROI success.

## Post 2

**Date:** Early 2026  

> **Updated study for anonymized queries, but note that this is April 2025 data, before AI Mode rolled out. I suspect the data is getting worse, and will have a study with more recent data soon...**
>
> If you saw Bing's recent announcement about AI search data in Bing Webmaster Tools, you may have noticed this line: "These phrases are grouped representations from your site that were retrieved and cited in AI-generated answers. They are not full user questions or prompts."
>
> There was no chance they'd ever give us all the data. They're low volume and unique, so most will be anonymized. The data is going to get worse in all the systems.

**Key Insight for AI-SEO Production:** As AI search grows, traditional keyword tracking tools (Google Search Console, Bing Webmaster Tools) will become increasingly opaque due to "anonymized queries" (long, conversational AI prompts). For 100Hires, this means we cannot rely solely on exact-match keyword data. We must shift our tracking to page-level conversions and brand search volume growth as proxies for AI visibility.
