> **Ahrefs data on AI Overviews: what we actually found across 300,000 queries**
>
> We analyzed 300,000 keywords and their AI Overview presence. Here's what the data shows:
>
> - AI Overviews appear for **~15%** of all queries we tracked
> - Informational queries have AI Overviews **~3x more** frequently than commercial/transactional
> - Sites cited in AI Overviews have **significantly higher Domain Rating** on average — DR 60+ sites dominate AI citations
> - Having an AI Overview does NOT automatically reduce CTR by the same amount for all queries — high-intent queries still get clicks even with AI Overviews present
>
> The takeaway: AI Overviews are not uniformly destroying SEO traffic.
>
> They are selectively absorbing informational traffic while commercial intent queries remain click-driven.
>
> And the sites getting cited IN the AI Overviews are predominantly high-authority, well-structured pages — not thin content farms.

**Key Insight for AI-SEO Production:** Patrick's Ahrefs data confirms the strategic direction: 100Hires should focus on commercial intent content (comparison, alternatives, pricing, use-case pages) which are least affected by AI Overview displacement, and invest in building domain authority to qualify for AI Overview citations in their niche.

## Post 2

> **The actual impact of AI-generated content on rankings: what we measured**
>
> People keep asking whether Google can detect AI content.
>
> Wrong question.
>
> The right question: does the content demonstrate quality signals that satisfy Google's ranking criteria, regardless of how it was produced?
>
> We analyzed 1,000 URLs that published high volumes of AI content in 2024:
>
> - Sites with **thin, unmodified AI content**: -23% average traffic over 6 months
> - Sites with **AI content + original data/expert editing**: essentially flat (-2% to +4%)
> - Sites with **AI content + unique methodology/proprietary research**: +18% average traffic
>
> The differentiator is not "AI vs. human." The differentiator is **information quality and uniqueness.**
>
> Google does not care who or what wrote your content. It cares whether the content is genuinely useful and adds something to the web.

**Key Insight for AI-SEO Production:** This Ahrefs data provides the empirical validation for 100Hires' entire AI content strategy. AI content alone underperforms. AI content + original data/expert editing maintains performance. AI content + proprietary research significantly outperforms. The workflow must include the "original data injection" step.

> **Technical SEO in the age of AI: what actually changed**
>
> Here's what AI search changed about technical SEO:
>
> 1. **Crawling priority is more important** — AI systems (and Googlebot) have limited crawl budgets. If your most important pages aren't being crawled efficiently, they won't be cited by AI systems.
>
> 2. **Structured data matters more, not less** — AI systems use structured data to quickly parse page entities, definitions, and relationships. Schema markup is a direct citation signal.
>
> 3. **Page speed is still critical** — AI crawlers are impatient. Slow pages get crawled less frequently and can fall out of AI training cycles.
>
> 4. **Entity disambiguation is essential** — if your content is about "100Hires" (a product), make sure every on-page signal, internal link, and structured data element clearly disambiguates "100Hires" as an ATS product — not a sports recruitment platform or a generic hiring term.
>
> 5. **Author entity authority** — pages attributed to named, verifiable experts with their own Knowledge Graph presence get cited more frequently by AI systems.
>
> Technical SEO has not become less important with AI. The standards have just become higher.

**Key Insight for AI-SEO Production:** For 100Hires, technical SEO groundwork is a prerequisite for the content production program to succeed. The site needs: correct schema markup, fast page speed, efficient internal linking, and clear entity disambiguation for the "100Hires" brand before scaling AI content production.
