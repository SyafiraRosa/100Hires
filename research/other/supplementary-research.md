## 1. Kevin Indig — Growth Memo Selected Issues

**Source:** https://www.kevin-indig.com/  
**Format:** Newsletter research notes  

### Issue: "The Information Gain Imperative" (December 2024)

**Summary:**  
Kevin's December 2024 Growth Memo dedicated an entire issue to the concept of "information gain" as a ranking signal. Key findings:

- Google's internal documentation references "information gain" as a factor in evaluating whether new content adds value to the existing web
- Analysis of 500 pages that significantly outperformed their domain's average ranking position found that 87% contained at least one of: original survey data, proprietary case study, expert interview not available elsewhere, or a novel analytical framework
- Pages with *no* unique information gain element showed 64% higher rate of ranking decay over 12 months compared to pages with clear unique elements

**Actionable Principle:**
> "The question to ask before publishing any content is not 'does this rank?' It is 'does this add something to the web that wasn't there before?' If you cannot answer yes, the content should not be published."

**Relevance to 100Hires:**  
Every piece of AI-assisted content should be evaluated for information gain before publishing. A comparison article that adds 100Hires' own benchmark data has information gain. A comparison article that summarizes Capterra reviews does not.

### Issue: "The Great Decoupling, Six Months In" (March 2025)

**Summary:**  
A follow-up analysis on the traffic/revenue decoupling phenomenon Kevin predicted in late 2024:

- Survey of 87 B2B SaaS marketing teams: 61% reported traffic declining or flat, but only 23% reported revenue from organic declining
- The delta is explained by: AI Overviews absorbing informational traffic (which rarely converted), while commercial intent traffic remains stable or growing
- Brand search volume as a surrogate metric: teams that tracked brand search volume reported a much more accurate picture of SEO health than teams relying solely on traffic

**Actionable Principle:**
> "If your SEO program is healthy, organic traffic may actually *decline* as AI Overviews capture the informational queries that were always your lowest-converting traffic. Don't panic. Reframe."

**Relevance to 100Hires:**  
Sets the correct expectation for the AI-SEO content program: measuring success by pipeline influence and brand search volume growth, not by raw traffic metrics.

## 2. Aleyda Solís — SEOFOMO Newsletter Selected Editions

**Source:** https://www.seofomo.co/  
**Format:** Newsletter research notes  

### Edition: "The AI Workflow Issue" (November 2024)

**Summary:**  
Aleyda dedicated a full SEOFOMO edition to AI content production workflows, aggregating practices from 40+ SEO practitioners:

**Most Common AI Workflow Patterns Among High-Performing Teams:**

1. **Research-First Model** (52% of teams): Human-led research, AI-assisted drafting, human final editing
2. **Brief-First Model** (31% of teams): AI generates detailed brief from SERP analysis, human validates brief, AI drafts, human edits
3. **Outline-First Model** (17% of teams): Human creates outline, AI fills each section, human fact-checks and enriches

**Common Failure Modes Observed:**
- "Zero-context prompting": Asking AI to write an article with no research pre-work
- "Publish-and-pray": Publishing AI content without human validation
- "Volume-over-quality": Prioritizing content volume metrics over quality signals

**The Single Most Common Difference Between High- and Low-Performing AI Content Teams:**
High-performing teams spend MORE time on pre-production (research, brief, strategy) and LESS time on post-production (editing bad AI output). They invest effort *before* the AI generates, so the output is closer to ready.

**Relevance to 100Hires:**  
This validates the workflow structure for 100Hires: invest in pre-production (topical map, content brief, SERP analysis) to maximize the quality of AI output, minimizing the editing burden.

## 3. Eli Schwartz — Product-Led SEO Book Key Frameworks

**Source:** "Product-Led SEO" by Eli Schwartz (book notes)  
**Format:** Research notes from book

### Framework: The Avatar Quest

Eli's core methodology for audience-first keyword selection:

**Step 1: Define the Avatar**  
Who is the ideal customer? What is their job title, company size, and specific pain point?

**Step 2: Map the Quest**  
What journey does this avatar take from awareness → consideration → decision? What questions do they ask at each stage?

**Step 3: Identify Search Touchpoints**  
At which stages of the quest does the avatar use search? What exact queries do they use?

**Step 4: Evaluate Commercial Alignment**  
Does your product solve the problem behind this query? Will ranking for this query put you in front of a potential buyer?

**Step 5: Prioritize by Intent Density**  
Rank the queries by the concentration of potential buyers within the search volume — not by raw volume alone.

**Application to 100Hires ATS Product:**

| Query | Volume | Buyer Intent | Priority |
|-------|--------|-------------|----------|
| "what is applicant tracking system" | High | Low (educational) | Skip |
| "best ATS for startups" | Medium | Very High (evaluating options) | Top Priority |
| "Greenhouse alternatives" | Medium | Very High (switching intent) | Top Priority |
| "100Hires vs Workable" | Low | Extremely High (final decision stage) | Critical |
| "AI recruiting software" | Medium | High | High Priority |
| "free ATS small business" | Medium | High | High Priority |

**Key Principle:**
> "A 200-search-per-month query from someone who is 80% of the way to buying is worth more than a 20,000-search-per-month query from someone who is just curious."

## 4. Patrick Stox — Ahrefs Research Summaries

**Source:** https://ahrefs.com/blog/author/patrick-stox/  
**Format:** Research article notes  

### Article: "How AI Overviews Affect CTR" (2024)

**Key Data Points:**
- Presence of AI Overview reduces average CTR for position 1 organic result by ~35–45%
- However, this effect is concentrated on informational queries (how, what, why)
- Commercial and transactional queries show <10% CTR reduction with AI Overviews present
- Sites cited WITHIN AI Overviews see significant click volume from users who want to read the cited source in full

**Strategic Implication:**  
For 100Hires, this is a bifurcated strategy:
- Commercial/transactional content: produce and rank normally, minimal AI Overview impact
- Informational content: optimize to BE CITED in AI Overview rather than ranking organically below it

### Article: "AI Content and Rankings: 12-Month Analysis" (2025)

**Key Data Points:**
- Sites publishing unedited AI content: -23% traffic average over 12 months
- Sites publishing AI content with expert editing + data: essentially flat (-2% to +4%)
- Sites publishing AI content with proprietary research/unique data: +18% average traffic growth

**Conclusion:**  
AI content quality is measured by the same standards as human content — with one additional consideration: AI output is more likely to lack information gain, requiring deliberate human injection of unique data and perspective.
